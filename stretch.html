Seated Cat-Cow Stretch

Instructions: Sit on the edge of your chair with feet flat on the floor. Inhale as you arch your back and look up (Cow). Exhale as you round your spine, tucking your chin to your chest (Cat).

Why it's beneficial: This gentle movement warms up the spine and helps relieve tension in the upper and lower back, which is common from prolonged sitting.

[AI-Generated Video]

Seated Spinal Twist

Instructions: Sit tall and place your right hand on the outside of your left knee. Place your left hand on the chair behind you. Gently twist your torso to the left. Hold, then repeat on the other side.

Why it's beneficial: Increases spinal mobility and can help alleviate lower back stiffness. It also provides a gentle stretch to the obliques and hips.
